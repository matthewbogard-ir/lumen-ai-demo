BRAND KNOWLEDGE:

Longines was founded in 1832 in Saint-Imier, Switzerland. The winged hourglass logo, registered in 1889, is the oldest unchanged trademark in the international register. Longines is part of the Swatch Group and is positioned in the premium segment of traditional Swiss watchmaking.

Brand pillars: Elegance, Tradition, Performance, Precision.

Longines has a heritage in sports timekeeping, serving as official timekeeper for equestrian sports, tennis, gymnastics, alpine skiing, and archery. The brand has roots in aviation through Charles Lindbergh's transatlantic flight.

Key technologies include in-house calibres developed with ETA, silicon balance springs for magnetic resistance, COSC-certified chronometer movements in the Spirit collection, column-wheel chronograph mechanisms, and 72-hour power reserve across most automatic models.

COLLECTIONS:

Master Collection: The art of classical watchmaking. Dress watches with complications like moon phase, chronograph, annual calendar, and GMT. Refined 40-42mm cases. Prices from $2,300 to $3,175.

Heritage: Living history on the wrist. Faithful reissues of iconic models from the brand's 190+ year archives. Highlights include the Legend Diver, Military COSD, Chronograph 1946, Diver 1967, and Sector Dial. Prices from $2,100 to $3,425.

Spirit: Born for adventure. Every Spirit watch is COSC-certified as a chronometer. Aviation heritage. Ranges from time-only models to the Zulu Time GMT and Flyback Chronograph. 100m water resistance. Prices from $2,350 to $4,050.

Conquest and HydroConquest: Performance and precision. Sporty elegance to serious dive capability. HydroConquest has 300m water resistance and ceramic bezels. The V.H.P. offers plus or minus 5 seconds per year quartz accuracy. Prices from $1,100 to $2,325.

Elegance: Timeless refinement. Includes the rectangular DolceVita, the ultra-slim La Grande Classique, and the jewelry-inspired PrimaLuna. Sizes from 21.5mm to 38.5mm, with both quartz and automatic options. Prices from $1,200 to $1,850.
